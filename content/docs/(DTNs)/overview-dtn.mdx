The **Decentralized Transaction Network (DTN)** is a core component of the Okto Layer, designed to automate and coordinate complex multi-chain transactions. By abstracting the complexities of multi-chain interactions, the DTN ensures that users can perform cross-chain operations seamlessly, without needing to manage each step manually.

---

## What is a DTN?

The DTN acts as the **"brain"** of the Okto Layer, coordinating all multi-chain transactions. It breaks down complex tasks into smaller subtasks, executes them in the correct sequence, and ensures that all steps are completed efficiently and securely.

### Key Features of DTNs

1. **Complex Orchestration**: The DTN can handle multi-step transactions, such as swapping tokens on one chain, bridging them to another chain, and staking them on the destination chain.

2. **On-Chain Verification**: Each step of the transaction is logged on the Okto Chain, ensuring transparency and trustlessness.

3. **Seamless Integration with DWN and ULL**: The DTN works closely with the **Decentralized Wallet Network (DWN)** for secure signing and the **Unified Liquidity Layer (ULL)** for efficient cross-chain swaps.

4. **Error Handling and Retries**: If a transaction fails due to network congestion or unexpected fees, the DTN automatically retries the operation.

5. **Asynchronous Capabilities**: The DTN can handle tasks across different chains happening at different times, ensuring everything stays coordinated.
